Task Progress & Bibliography Information FY2018 
Task Progress: • lead author in a recently published paper analyzing the collection of anti-microbial genes (“resistome”) found on the ISS using a variety of molecular techniques.

• performed whole genome sequencing on 4 fungal isolates (Fusarium oxysporum) isolated from the ISS during MT-1 and VEGGIE projects. Performed comparative genomics and single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) analysis on these genomes and believes that at least two may be a novel strain.

• analyzed the microbial load and microbial diversity of ISS surface samples collected during Microbial Tracking-1 and is a co-author on a paper that is under review by mSphere.

• provided invaluable help to colleagues in processing samples from a variety of Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and NASA collaborative projects.

• is a Co-Investigator (Co-I) and helped extensively in the write up of the recently awarded grant: “Omics in Space (OIS): Technology Development for Omics Instrumentations and Biomolecule Measurement”

• developed a project here at JPL to assess the impact of microgravity on the rate of horizontal gene transfer between different bacterial genera. Preliminary results are very interesting and more work is being conducted.

• wrote and submitted a ROSBio grant (as a Co-I) to help support work on the impact of microgravity on anti-microbial resistance and virulence.

• presented her research in oral presentations at American Society for Gravitational and Space Research-ASGSR (Cleveland and Seattle), AbSciCon (Mesa, Arizona), an International Astronautical Congress (Australia) meetings.

• Postdoc strives to keep her skills current and took a week-long course in RNA-seq analysis.

• MT-2 project: has processed: (a) SpaceX Dragon surface and air samples, (b) processed ISS surface and air samples collected from two flight sampling events, (c) performed culture and molecular analysis to measure microbial burden, (d) archived DNA and microbial isolates, and (e) coordinated with collaborating institutions like Lawrence Livermore, Johnson Space Center-JSC, and NASA Ames for sample analyses. She prepared debriefings on numerous occasions for all those involved in the project. In addition to the environmental samples, she has processed crew samples collected from two subjects to date.
